# Crystal Huang Dance Website Visual Upgrade Design

## Overview

This redesign transforms the current site from a polished student-style dance portfolio into a curated digital dossier for a young artist with elite training and international distinction. The primary audience is split between professional review contexts and commercial or collaboration contexts, with the information architecture intentionally weighted toward review, audition, and credibility.

The chosen direction is `Modern Performance Archive` executed through a `Curated Dossier` approach. The site should feel refined, international, editorial, and trustworthy. It should read as a formal artist portfolio first and a promotional website second.

## Goals

- Present Crystal Huang as a serious emerging artist rather than a generic dance student.
- Help reviewers understand her caliber within the first few seconds.
- Preserve artistic atmosphere without sacrificing readability or scan speed.
- Improve the sense of restraint, authorship, and premium presentation.
- Keep the site usable for professional inquiries, collaborations, and general public discovery.

## Non-Goals

- Reposition the site as a high-conversion commercial landing page.
- Add large amounts of new content or new backend features.
- Create a noisy, over-animated, or trend-driven portfolio aesthetic.
- Treat every achievement, media asset, or dance style as equal in hierarchy.

## Experience Principles

- `Authority first`: the site should immediately communicate level, training, and distinction.
- `Curated over crowded`: fewer, stronger visual moments beat dense grids.
- `Editorial clarity`: typography and spacing should make the content feel reviewed and intentional.
- `Archive logic`: the navigation and sectioning should feel like a structured body of work.
- `Quiet confidence`: motion and decoration should support the work, not compete with it.

## Visual Direction

### Palette

Use a restrained archival palette:

- Bone or warm white for light surfaces and major text contrast zones.
- Ink black and charcoal for backgrounds and strong anchors.
- Subtle gray tonal steps for dividers and metadata.
- Aged gold or oxblood as a sparing accent for emphasis and distinction.

The redesign should significantly reduce the current reliance on saturated blue-purple gradients. Color should feel museum-grade, not template-like.

### Typography

Use a high-contrast editorial system:

- Headings and name treatments should use a refined serif or display serif tone.
- Navigation, metadata, labels, and body copy should use a clean sans-serif.
- Year markers, distinctions, and institutional references should feel typeset, not promotional.

The typography must create a dossier-like mood while preserving fast readability in both English and Chinese.

### Layout And Spacing

- Expand white space around major content blocks.
- Reduce the number of equally weighted cards on screen.
- Use stronger margins, cleaner horizontal rhythms, and fewer decorative containers.
- Introduce section transitions that feel like moving through a catalog or exhibition archive.

### Motion

Motion should be understated and elegant:

- Fade-ins, upward drifts, image reveals, and subtle mask transitions.
- Minimal hover theatrics.
- No flashy glow-heavy animations or overt tech-product microinteractions.

## Homepage Structure

The homepage will be restructured into the following sequence.

### 1. Cover Frame

The opening screen functions as a dossier cover rather than a marketing hero.

Content:

- Crystal Huang name treatment as the dominant visual anchor.
- A concise professional identity line instead of a broad inspirational tagline.
- One strongest primary image used as the cover visual.
- Supporting metadata presented with restraint.

The emotional goal is: this is a serious young artist worthy of formal consideration.

### 2. Identity Strip

Directly beneath the cover frame, include a compact line or band of highest-signal credentials.

Examples:

- San Francisco Ballet School Trainee
- ABT JKO School
- Prix de Lausanne Prize Winner
- Contemporary Dance Award

This strip replaces generic descriptive copy and establishes credibility quickly.

### 3. Selected Distinctions

Feature two to four top distinctions as large, deliberate editorial blocks rather than a generic feature grid.

Each block should emphasize one major institutional or competitive signal. The goal is to surface the strongest proof points before the user reaches the full archive timeline.

### 4. Profile Preview

Present a concise artist profile preview instead of the entire long biography at once.

This area should introduce artistic identity, training depth, and worldview in short readable paragraphs. It should feel like a formal artist statement, not a casual self-introduction.

### 5. Archive Entry Points

Use a structured section or linked matrix to guide the user into the site's major archive areas:

- Training
- Awards
- Repertoire or Artistic Range
- Media

This creates a stronger information architecture and supports fast scanning.

### 6. Professional Inquiry

Preserve a contact entry point, but frame it for auditions, collaborations, bookings, teaching, and professional inquiries. It should appear deliberate and formal rather than sales-oriented.

## Section-Level Changes

### Navbar

- Simplify the navigation into a thin, quiet archive-style top bar.
- Retain bilingual switching.
- Reduce the marketing feel of the CTA.
- Favor labels such as `Profile`, `Archive`, `Distinctions`, `Media`, and `Contact`.

### Hero To Cover Frame

- Replace the current motivational hero treatment with a dossier cover format.
- Remove emphasis on generic inspirational quote language as the centerpiece.
- Prioritize name, identity, institutional references, and one powerful image.

### About To Artist Profile

- Reframe `About` as `Artist Profile`.
- Break long text into two or three concise paragraphs.
- Preserve achievement depth while improving scan speed.

### Dance Styles To Repertoire Or Artistic Range

- Recast dance styles as a professional artistic range overview.
- Prioritize the styles with strongest visual support.
- Convert entries without image assets into a more restrained list or metadata presentation so empty image slots never cheapen the design.

### Achievements To Selected Distinctions Plus Archive Timeline

- Split the current achievement presentation into a highlighted top layer and a full archive layer.
- Major distinctions should appear as featured editorial elements.
- Remaining awards should appear in a structured timeline or record format.

### Videos To Featured Performance Reels

- Present videos as evidence of performance range and professional readiness.
- Improve titling, grouping, and framing so the section feels curatorial rather than embedded-by-default.

### Gallery To Curated Stills

- Reduce the sense of a generic photo gallery.
- Use fewer images if needed, with stronger sequencing and layout rhythm.
- Favor representational quality over asset count.

### Contact To Professional Inquiries

- Reframe language toward formal inquiry categories.
- Maintain accessibility for general outreach while making professional uses feel primary.

### Footer

- Simplify the footer into a clean data-style close.
- Preserve essential social and contact references.
- Avoid decorative excess.

## Content Guidance

- Replace broad motivational messaging with professional identity language.
- Ensure achievements are ranked by significance rather than simple volume.
- Avoid filler phrasing and promotional clichés.
- Maintain a bilingual experience that feels equally intentional in both languages.

## Photography Guidance

Assign roles to imagery rather than treating every image as interchangeable:

- `Cover image`: primary authority-building portrait or performance still.
- `Archival still`: supporting evidence in gallery or archive contexts.
- `Detail crop`: visual punctuation between data-heavy sections.
- `Supporting image`: secondary material used to vary rhythm without weakening focus.

Where image assets are weak or missing, prefer a text-forward archival layout instead of stretching low-signal imagery.

## Testing And Acceptance Criteria

The redesign should be considered successful when:

- A first-time visitor understands Crystal's level and positioning within the first screen.
- Desktop and mobile both retain a premium dossier feeling.
- Bilingual switching does not break layout balance or hierarchy.
- Hero, distinctions, archive entry points, and media are all legible in a quick skim.
- Sections no longer feel like a generic portfolio template.
- The site reads as a curated artist archive suitable for both review and professional inquiry.

## Implementation Notes

During implementation, preserve existing valuable content and data where possible, but rewrite structure and presentation aggressively enough to achieve the new positioning. The redesign is not a skin-only pass. It is a hierarchy, tone, and editorial system upgrade.
